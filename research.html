Research

Research Themes & Technical Direction

My research focuses on building AI systems that combine strong predictive performance with transparency and reliability. I am especially interested in the intersection of transformer-based learning, explainable AI, and real-world decision contexts such as healthcare informatics, image forensics, and information credibility assessment.

Theme 1: Trustworthy AI & Explainability

I work on interpretability and trust-building methods that help users understand what a model learned, why it made a prediction, and where it can fail.

  • Explainability: Grad-CAM and visual attribution for CNN-based forensic pipelines.
  • Confidence-aware analysis: analyzing uncertainty, decision thresholds, and error types.
  • Reliability: evaluation under class imbalance and real-world distribution shifts.

Theme 2: Transformers for Text, Vision & Video

Transformer architectures provide powerful sequence modeling for text, images, and video. I use them for classification, representation learning, and spatiotemporal understanding.

  • NLP: BERT-family models for credibility and sentiment tasks.
  • Video: TimeSformer-style modeling for fine-grained soccer action recognition.
  • Training: fine-tuning pretrained models with controlled optimization and evaluation.

Theme 3: Healthcare Informatics & Biomedical AI

I apply machine learning and data-driven methods to biomedical and health-related datasets to support improved understanding of disease mechanisms and inform clinical research workflows.

  • Biomedical data preprocessing, feature engineering, and analysis.
  • Integrating ML approaches with bioinformatics tools and statistical workflows.
  • Emphasis on explainability and responsible AI in health contexts.

Theme 4: Information Quality, Credibility & Governance

I study credibility assessment in information systems by combining model-based signals with transparent validation logic. This includes fake news detection and source credibility analysis.

  • Credibility classification and explainable decision support outputs.
  • Rule-based validation + model inference for transparent scoring.
  • Data governance themes: privacy, accountability, and ethical compliance.

Technical Competencies (Research-Oriented)

Modeling & Evaluation

  • Supervised & unsupervised learning; transfer learning; hyperparameter tuning
  • Class imbalance handling and metric-driven evaluation (AUC, recall, accuracy)
  • Experiment tracking mindset: baselines → ablations → error analysis
